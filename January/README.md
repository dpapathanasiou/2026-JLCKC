# 1, 2 [搗ち合う](<./2026 JANUARY 1-2.pdf>)

### かちあう

> to clash; to collide; to conflict; to coincide​  
> to meet (by chance); to come across; to run into​

- 物事が偶然重なる。
    > Things just happen to coincide.
- 「来客が搗ち合う」などと使う。
    > It is used in expressions such as "guests come into contact with each other."
-  臼で餅などを搗くとき、株と株がぶつかり合う意。
    > It means that when pounding rice cakes or other foods in a mortar, the pieces of rice bump into each other.
- 転じて、物と物がぶつかり合う意になった。
    > By extension, it has come to mean things colliding with each other.

# 3, 4 [韋駄天](<./2026 JANUARY 3-4.pdf>)

### いだてん

> a great runner​  
> Skanda (swift-footed guardian deity) - ​Buddhism

- 足の速い人。
    > A fast runner.
- 「韋駄天走り」などと使う。
    > It is used in expressions such as "Idaten Hashiri".
- 「韋駄天」は、足の速いことで知られる神のこと。
    > "Idaten" is a god known for his speed.
- もとはバラモン教（代インドの民族宗教）の神で、のちに仏教の守護神になった。
    > He was originally a god of Brahmanism (the ethnic religion of ancient India) and later became the patron deity of Buddhism.

# 5, 6, 7 [火花を散らす](<./2026 JANUARY 5-7.pdf>)

### ひばなをちらす

> to give off sparks; to spark​  
> to have a heated argument; to fight fiercely

- たがいに激しく争う。
    > Fighting fiercely against each other.
- 「火花」は、石や金属などが激しくぶつかったときに出る火。
    > A "spark" is the fire that is produced when objects such as stones or metals collide violently.
- 戦いの場で、たがいの刀を火花が散るほど激しくぶつけ合うところから生まれた表現。
    > This expression originated from the scene of two swords clashing fiercely against each other on the battlefield, creating sparks.

# 8, 9 [お題目](<./2026 JANUARY 8-9.pdf>)

### おだいもく

> (an empty) slogan​  
> Nichiren chant

- くり返し唱えられること。
    > Something that is repeated over and over again.
- 口先だけで実現できそうもない主張。
    > Claims that seem impossible to achieve with mere words.
- 「お題目を唱える」などと使う。
    > It is used in phrases such as "to chant the Buddhist mantra."
- 「題目」の丁寧語で、日蓮宗で唱える「南無妙法蓮華経」の七目次を指す。
    > This is the polite form of the word "title," and it refers to the seven-part invocation of "Namu Myoho Renge Kyo" chanted in the Nichiren sect of Buddhism.
- 南無妙法蓮華経は、「法華経」の表題「妙法蓮華経」に「南無」を加えたもので、「法 華 経」の教えに帰依する（よ りすがる）という意。
    > "Namu Myoho Renge Kyo" is formed by adding "Namu" to the title "Myoho Renge Kyo" of the "Lotus Sutra," and it signifies devotion to (or reliance on) the teachings of the "Lotus Sutra."

# 10, 11 [べらぼう](<./2026 JANUARY 10-11.pdf>)

> extreme; terrible; excessive  
> absurd; unreasonable; foolish  
> fool; idiot​

- ばかげていること。
    > That's ridiculous.
- また、程度がはなはだしこと。
    > Furthermore, the situation is extremely serious.
- 江戸時代の見せ物で評判になった「可坊」からとされ。
    > The name is believed to have originated from "Kabō," a character who became famous in Edo period sideshows.
- 「蓖棒」と書くのは当て字。
    > Writing it as "蓖棒" is a phonetic substitution using unrelated characters.
