# 1, 2, 3 [豈図らんや](<./2026 JUNE 1-3.pdf>)

### あにはからんや

> contrary to one's expectations; to one's surprise​

- 思いがけず。
    > Unexpectedly.
- 意外にも。
    >  Surprisingly. 
- もとは、「どうしてそんなことを予測できようか（いや予測できない）」の意。
    > Originally, it meant "*how could anyone have predicted such a thing?* (or rather, *it was impossible to predict*)".
- 「あに」は、漢文の「豈」を訓読したもので、文頭について反語の意を表すことは。
    > The Japanese reading of the classical Chinese character "豈" is "*ani*", and when placed at the beginning of a sentence, it expresses a rhetorical question.
- 「予想外である」という気持ちを、その「あに」を用いて漢文調にいったもの。
    > It is a way of expressing the feeling of "*this is unexpected*" in a classical Chinese literary style.


# 4, 5 [月並み](<./2026 JUNE 4-5.pdf>)

### つきなみ

> trite; commonplace; conventional; hackneyed

- ありふれたものの形容。平凡なさま。
    > A description of something commonplace or ordinary.
- もとは毎月定期的に行うこと。
    > Originally, it referred to something done regularly every month.
- とくに、俳句などの月例会を「月並会」といった。
    > In particular, the monthly gatherings for haiku and other forms of poetry were called "*tsukinami-kai*", or monthly meetings.
- 伝統的な旧派の平凡な俳句を、正岡子規が「月並調」と批判したことから。
    > This term originated from Masaoka Shiki's criticism of traditional and trite haiku as being "*tsukinami-cho*", or monthly style.

# 6, 7 [普請](<./2026 JUNE 6-7.pdf>)

### ふしん

> building; construction  
> group effort by Buddhist practitioners; group activities by a community

- 家などを建てること。
    > It refers to the act of constructing buildings, such as houses. 
- 「安普請」は、安い費用や粗末な材料で建てた家のこと。
    > The term "*yasubushin*", which means cheap construction, denotes a house built at low cost or with inferior materials.
- 功徳をひろく請い願うことを「普請」といい、禅宗の寺で、修行僧が総出で労働に従事することをいった。
    > Originally, "*fushin*" referred to the act of earnestly seeking spiritual merit; in Zen temples, it described occasions when all the trainee monks would turn out to engage in manual labor together.
- 転じて、室町時代から江戸時代には、築城の際の大掛かりな土木工事のことを意味した。
    > Subsequently, from the Muromachi through the Edo periods, the term came to signify large-scale civil engineering projects involved in castle construction.

# 8, 9, 10 [縒りを戻す](<./2026 JUNE 8-10.pdf>)

### よりをもどす

> to untangle; to untwine​  
> to get back together with a former partner

- 別れた男女がまた一緒になること。
    > When a couple who had separated gets back together.
- 「縒り」は、糸などをねじり合わせること。
    > The word "*yori*" refers to the act of twisting threads or similar materials together.
- もつれた男女関係を糸に見立て、それを元の状態に戻すということから。
    > The expression likens a tangled romantic relationship to a tangled thread and describes the process of restoring it to its original state.

# 11, 12 [搦め手](<./2026 JUNE 11-12.pdf>)

### からめて

> rear gate of a castle; back entrance  
> an opponent's weak point​

- 相手の注意の向かいてない部分。弱点。
    > It refers to an area where an opponent's attention is not focused; a weak point.
- 「搦め手から批判する」などと使う。
    >  It is used in phrases such as "*launching an attack from the rear*".
- 城の正門の「大手」に対し、裏門を「搦め手」という。
    > While the main gate of a castle is called the "*ōte*", the rear gate is known as the "*karamete*".
- また、裏門から攻める軍勢のことも「搦め手」という。
    > The term also refers to an army attacking from the rear flank. 
- 転じて、物事の裏側や相手の目の行き届かない部分という意になった。
    > By extension, it came to signify the hidden side of things, or areas that escape an opponent's notice.

# 13, 14 [衒う](<./2026 JUNE 13-14.pdf>)

### てらう

> to show off; to parade; to pretend​

- 自分の才能などをひけらかす。
    > To show off one's talents or abilities.
- 「奇を衒う」などと使う。
    > Used in phrases such as "to be flashy," or "to be pretentious."
- 「照を衒う」の意で、物をはっきり見せるようにすることから。
    > It originates from the phrase "to be shining brightly," as an indication of something becoming more visible.

# 15, 16, 17 [杓子定規](<./2026 JUNE 15-17.pdf>)

### しゃくしじょうぎ

> strict; hidebound; inflexible; being a stickler for the rules; sticking hard and fast to the rules

- 決まった形式にとらわれて、応用や融通がきかないこと。
    > Being bound by a fixed format and lacking adaptability or flexibility.
- 「杓子」は、汁や飯を器によそう道具。
    > The first part of the expression is "ladle," a tool for serving soup or rice into a bowl.
- 古くはその柄が曲がっていたことから、「杓子定規」は不適切な基準で物事をはかろうとする意だった。
    > Because its handle was curved, using a "ladle ruler" was an old expression meaning to measure things with an inappropriate standard.
- それが転じて、適切であろうとなかろうと、一つの基準で物事をかろうとする意になった。
    > This phrase has evolved to mean trying to measure things by a single standard, regardless of whether or not it is appropriate.
